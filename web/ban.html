People's Republic of Bangladesh

Bangladesh, officially the People's Republic of Bangladesh,is a country in South Asia. It is the eighth-most populous country in the world and is among the most densely populated countries with a population of nearly 174 million in an area of 148,460 square kilometres (57,320 sq mi). Bangladesh shares land borders with India to the west, north, and east, and Myanmar to the southeast; to the south, it has a coastline along the Bay of Bengal. It is narrowly separated from Bhutan and Nepal by the Siliguri Corridor and from China by the Indian state of Sikkim in the north. Dhaka, the capital and largest city, is the nation's political, financial, and cultural centre. Chittagong, the second-largest city, is the busiest port on the Bay of Bengal. The official language is Bengali.

Places to Visit in Bangladesh

Sundarbans: The Mangrove Marvel of South Asia

Venture into the Sundarbans, the crown jewel of mangrove forests in South Asia and a UNESCO World Heritage marvel in Bangladesh. Teeming with an array of wildlife, including the legendary Bengal tiger and a myriad of avian species, this is far from your run-of-the-mill woodland adventure. For an added sprinkle of natural wonder, hop on a boat through Ratargul Swamp Forest, a migratory bird's paradise. It's a festival of biodiversity you'd regret missing.
Best Time to Visit: November to February
Entry Fees: A cool INR 570; for one-day boat safari, it is INR 2,499
Dress Code:Think comfort meets jungle chic—humidity is the name of the game here

Cox's Bazar: The Longest Natural Sea Beach

Discover Cox's Bazar, the world record-holder for the longest natural sea beach, boasting a staggering 120 kilometers of sandy bliss in southeastern Bangladesh. Famous for its breathtaking sunsets and killer waves, this beach nirvana beckons surfers and sunbathers, flip-flop fans of every type. Want more? Make a quick jaunt to Chhera Island, just an hour's speedboat ride away, for an underwater adventure featuring snorkeling and diving amid untamed natural beauty.
Entry Fees: Free as the ocean breeze
Best Time to Visit: November to March

Dhaka: The Heart of Bangladesh

Meet Dhaka — the pulsating heart of Bangladesh whose beat is an echo of its historical grace and a reflection of its contemporary vigor. Explore architectural marvels like Lalbagh Fort and soul-stirring sanctuaries like Dhakeshwari Temple. Don't overlook the jaw-dropping design of Curzon Hall or the national spiritual center, Baitul Mukarram. For the history buffs, a visit to the Bangladesh National Museum is non-negotiable. Shopping aficionado? Dhaka offers everything from luxury malls like Bashundhara City to artisanal crafts.

Srimangal: The Tea Capital

TIndulge in the finest teas at Srimangal, affectionately known as Bangladesh's 'Tea Capital.' Nestled in the Sylhet District, this tranquil town is a verdant sea of sprawling tea estates. Immerse yourself in a sensory retreat amid undulating hills and the soothing sounds of nature. Don't miss a bird-watching escapade at Baikka Beel and trace the journey of tea from leaf to cup with an enlightening plantation tour.
Best Time to Visit: November to March
Entry Fees: Mostly free, but tea tasting tours may cost extra

Gaur: The Ancient City of Rich History and Architectural Beauty

Journey to Gaur, an ancient city situated in Bangladesh's Bogra District, celebrated for its rich tapestry of history and archaeological marvels that date back to the 8th century. It’s a magnet for history buffs and offers everything from intricate medieval Hindu temples (think Kanta jew temple) to ancient Buddhist sanctuaries. Just a 2-3 hour drive from Dhaka, Gaur offers a range of lodging options, from luxury hotels to more budget-friendly stays. The city is a frequent feature in exhibits at the Bangladesh National Museum.
Best Time to Visit: November to February
Entry Fees: Generally free, but guided tours may cost extra

Most Popular Festivals

Durga Puja

Durga Puja is an annual festival for Hindu Believers. This occasion comes at the Hindu month of ‘Ashvin’ and lasts for 4 days. As you would expect, the event consists of family and social gatherings. Gifting to each other, lighting, and decorating houses are very common. People worship their Goddess Durga inside temples. They perform ancient religious singing and dancing and paste powder color on each other’s faces to bring the celebration mode on. Eventually, the process ends with idol immersion in the river.

Eid Ul Fitr

Eid Ul Fitr: This is the most joyful occasion for all Muslims across the country. Even the non-Muslims like to join the celebration of Eid with everyone. The holy day comes after the month of Ramadan where the Muslim believers performed fasting for 30 days. It is believed that Eid Day is a blessing from Allah to enjoy the achievement after a long 30 days of fasting. In the morning, all the mosques and fields are filled with people to perform the Eid Salat. Prior to that, they take a shower, get ready wearing new Pajama-Panjabi, and apply perfume.

Buddha Purnima

Buddha Purnima is celebrated by Buddhists descent of the country to celebrate the birthday of Gautam Buddha. They observe it annually according to the Gregorian calendar. On this day, the Buddhist temples are decorated. A fair is held outside of temples. Also, some music containing Buddha’s message is played. Finally, the believers attend the prayer, light candles and recite the 3 jewels and 5 precepts.

Nouka Baich

Nouka Baich (boat rowing) is a traditional sport that is observed in rural areas. The participants of the same team, ranging from 10 to 20 passengers, ride in a long boat and paddle firmly to put their boat ahead of other participatory teams. This game occurs in the large rivers and people generally organize this kind of fun due to any local or national level success. Thousands of people gather to witness this program. There is prize money for the winner also, which is handed over by the local Chairman.

Pohela Baishakh

One of the cultures that Bengali people have been following with zeal for centuries is Pahela Baishakh. This is the 1st day of Bengali New Year and the most popular occasion of Bangladesh. Both men and women are dressed in red and white. Girls wear Saree with colorful bangles and boys wear Panjabi. Various activities are held in many places throughout the day. Bengali foods like Panta Ilish (Hilsha fish and rice), various Pithas (cakes), Candy floss, etc, are presented in front of the visitors at the fair. Nagordola (Ferris wheel) is set up for amusement. Meanwhile, folk songs are played under Bot-tola (Banyan tree).

Pohela Falgun

Pahela Falgun is one of the most popular days in the Bengali Culture. Generally, this day is on the 13th of February of the year. This is the 1st day of spring. People welcome this day wearing yellow and white. Some people buy flowers and present those to their loved ones. Bengali women set flower crowns around their heads. They like to use a variety of flowers, but mainly Marigold, as it has the color of Spring. The most beautiful Spring song “Basanta Esche Geche” (The Spring has arrived) is played everywhere from the morning. Cultural Institutes organize programs like dance shows and song performances.

Food Culture in Bangladesh

Panta Ilish

Panta Ilish is a traditional food that almost every Bangladeshi eats at the Pohela Boishakh Festival (the first day of Bangla year). It’s the most popular food in Bangladesh. We love this dish as it is a great symbol and celebration of our culture and tradition.

Kacchi Biryani

The most popular biryani in Bangladesh is Dhaka Kacchi Biryani. You will always find the Dhaka street biryani stall crowded because of the huge demand for this food. The main ingredients are rice and marinated meat cooked in lots of spices, giving it its special taste. The spices are nutmeg, mace, pepper, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, bay leaves, coriander, mint, ginger, onion, tomatoes, green chilies, and garlic. It is sometimes served with a boiled egg and salad.

Grill Chicken With Naan Roti

Almost every restaurant in Bangladesh serves this dish, and more than 50,000 grilled chickens are sold every day throughout the country. There are different ways of grilling chicken and there are many variations of spices that are used. Many restaurants roast chickens in a glass oven placed outside to entice people in to eat. It’s a very successful strategy.

Puchka

Probably the most loved snack and street food in the whole of Bangladesh is Puchka. It originated in India but has become popular in Bangladesh, Pakistan, and Nepal. Puchka is round or ball-shaped deep-fried, crisp flatbread which is served with mashed potato, dal, chaat masala, onion, and coriander.

Mughlai

This Bengali street food comes from the time of the Mughal Empire, where it is believed to have been prepared for the royal court of Mughal Emperor Jahangir. Mughlai is generally soft fried bread or paratha stuffed with kemma (minced meat), wheat flour, ghee, eggs, onions, green chili and oil. Moghlai is a popular snack

Rasgulla

Rasgulla or Roshogulla (syrup filled roll) is one of the most popular sweets in Bangladesh. It is traditionally eaten after lunch as the people of Bangladesh love to eat sweet dishes after a heavy meal. Sweet and spongy rasgulla is also served to guests and at festivals and special occasions.
